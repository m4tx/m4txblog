I recently went to the United Kingdom for a week-long road trip. As a person who likes driving, and a person who drove in a few different, mostly European, countries, I have a few thoughts I would like to share in hoping they will be interesting!

## Driving on the ~~wrong~~ correct side of the road

Historically, there used to be more countries having left-hand traffic. There are many myths explaining why was that, with the one I've heard most often was that people on the horses rode on the left so that in an event of an enemy encounter, they could immediately get their sword into their dominant hand (meaning right for 90% of the population) and fight with their enemy on their right. French Colonial Empire reversed the sidedness many of such countries.

Currently, two thirds of the world drive on the right hand side, which means that most likely you, the reader, is used to driving on the right side as well. Therefore, the most obvious difference will be switching to the left hand side.

Interestingly, I don't really think this makes much of a difference, though. I've heard many different opinions about this and most likely I have my own bias (as mentioned, I really enjoy driving). After an hour or two, there really isn't much difference anymore. Your brain is able to adapt quickly and mirroring all the traffic rules is easier than it sounds. What probably also helps is that people in the UK drive slowly and carefully, much more cautiously than Poland, for instance.
